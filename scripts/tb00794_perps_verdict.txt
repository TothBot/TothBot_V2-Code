regime-gated SHORT (spot couldn't) and change the best LONG?

ANSWER: YES, shorts revive decisively - BUT the dominant lever is the perp FEE
(0.05% vs 0.26%/side), not funding. Funding is a small SHORT tailwind and a
real LONG headwind. The best LONG shifts toward breakout entries; the
carry-forward emacross long still works.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. WHAT ACTUALLY REVIVED THEM: FEE >> FUNDING (the honest decomposition)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The focused carry-forward sweep prints funding per trade in bps (fund_bp) and
E with funding zeroed (E_noFund), isolating funding's contribution:

  - SHORT funding credit:  -2 to -15 bps/trade (shorts COLLECT positive funding).
  - LONG funding drag:    +14 to +145 bps/trade (longs PAY funding; grows with
                          hold time - ~115-145 bps/trade on slow 24h longs).
  - FEE saving vs spot:   0.52% -> 0.10% round-trip = ~42 bps/trade, BOTH sides.

So the revival math for shorts is ~42 bps (fee) + ~5-15 bps (funding credit)
= ~50 bps/trade of cost removed vs the spot model. The funding credit alone is
too small to revive shorts; the FEE is the load-bearing lever. Proof: the BARE
trend-following carry-forward short (emacross/momentum + wide stop + reversal,
no filter, no trail) stays FLAT on perps too -

  Engine B  emacross SHORT  4h/8h:  ~0.00%/tr,  ci~0  (NOT revived)
            momentum SHORT  4h/8h:  negative,   ci~0  (NOT revived)

Funding did NOT fix trend-following shorts. The perp instrument's low fee
revived the MEAN-REVERSION / filtered + trailing shorts that fees had erased.

The TB00786 carry-forward LONG (slow + wide 2-2.5xATR + reversal) TRANSFERS and

   8h   bb_break/trend100 + wide stop + rev  +3.04%/tr  rr3.36  SIG (n=999)
   12h  donchian/trend100 + wide stop + rev  +3.75%/tr  rr3.90  SIG (n=643)

emacross/donchian/bb_break all clear; bb_break + a trend/vol filter is the

NEW honest caveat for longs: funding is a real HEADWIND that scales with hold
time. Slow-tf perp longs pay ~1-1.5% of notional per trade in funding. The edge
survives because the gross move is large, but funding is not negligible and a
fee-only model would overstate the slow-long edge.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. SKEPTICAL CAVEATS (TB00786-honest)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
a. FEE IS LOAD-BEARING. The entire short revival rides on 0.05% perp taker. The
   short edge is THIN (+1.5%/tr, rr<2) and cost-sensitive: Engine B short cells
   flip negative by ~10-20 bp of added slippage. If the live Kraken-perp taker /
   effective spread is worse than 0.05%/side, the short edge erodes first. Longs
   are far more robust to cost.
b. ALT-SEASON INFLATION. The 24h LONG +8-15%/tr is inflated by the 2021/2024 alt
   bull (same caveat as TB00786 spot). Honest forward is the 8h/12h band
   (+1.5-3%/tr). 24h trade counts are small (n~265).
c. SELECTION RISK. Engine A short survivors come from a 525-combo search;
   multiple-comparison risk applies. Mitigated (not eliminated) by nested
   IS/OOS + regime-robust + bootstrap, AND by the fact that 40-63 survive and
   cluster on ONE coherent mechanism (mean-rev + trail + filter), not 1-2 random
   cells.
d. PERP HISTORY ~5-6yr (younger than spot's 7.5yr); some alts only ~2.5yr. The
   8-era test spans 2021 bull / 2022 bear / 2023-25 and is meaningful, but the
   deep-bear sample is thinner than spot's.
e. FUNDING REGIME RISK. Shorts collect funding only while funding is positive
   (the crypto-bull norm). In a sustained negative-funding (bear-led) regime the
   short tailwind becomes a headwind; the fee advantage persists regardless.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

   2-5xATR stop + run-to-reversal, slow tf (8-24h). The emacross carry-forward
   long still works. Funding is a new, non-trivial long headwind.
