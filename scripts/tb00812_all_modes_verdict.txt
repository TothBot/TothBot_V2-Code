THE READS (honest, no hedging language)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. SPOT LONG is by far the biggest engine (+$19,688, Calmar 16.1) - universe size (62 pairs) + trade
   count (2380). But it has NO downside protection and is the WORST hit in the current winter (-$718,
   -6.24% E, 15% win). It is a SUMMER instrument (E +23.7% bull vs +5.6% bear).
2. ADDING A HEDGE IMPROVES RISK-ADJUSTED RETURN on both sides (at the light 30% overlay):
   - Long + short hedge (M3) Calmar 11.8 > long-perp-only (M2) 8.3, and profit is slightly HIGHER
     (+$4,760 vs +$4,259) - the hedge here is better than free.
   - Short + long hedge (M5) Calmar 5.3 > short-only (M4) 2.2, profit +$2,541 vs +$1,353 - the long
     overlay nearly doubles the short book's risk-adjusted return.
3. THE CURRENT WINTER is the decisive signal for the on/off call:
   - Pure long books BLEED: spot -$718, long-perp -$98.
   - The SHORT is the side that EARNS right now: short-only +$241 (62% win, Calmar 2.5).
   - The HEDGED books are protected: long+short-hedge -$25 (vs -$98 naked), short+long-hedge +$212.
   => Bill's instinct NOT to run long-only in this winter is CORRECT. But "neither side does well now"
      is only HALF right: the LONG doesn't, the SHORT does, and the hedged books are roughly flat-to-up.
4. SUMMER vs WINTER (the switch): longs dominate summer (spot E +23.7%), the short is a thin all-weather
   edge that is RELATIVELY best in winter/cooling. So the cycle call is real - but TB00811 already showed
   that MANUALLY flipping the switch underperforms just running BOTH sides always (the per-pair filters
   already time it), and TB00801a/cycle-strategy-verdict reached the same conclusion. The hedge captures
   the cycle by running both, not by an on/off switch.

BOTTOM LINE
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Best standalone engine = SPOT LONGS (but summer-only; bleeding now). Best risk-adjusted perp books =
  the HEDGED modes (M3 long+short-hedge Calmar 11.8; M5 short+long-hedge Calmar 5.3), each beating its
  naked side. Right NOW (winter): longs lose, the short earns, hedged books are protected. The data
  supports running BOTH sides with a light hedge overlay over manually switching a single side on/off.
